HOLISTIC APPROACH TO ASSESS THE OVERALL HEALTH STATUS OF THE ENVIRONMENT REGARDING THE ACTUAL AND LONG LAST PRESENCE OF EMERGING CONTAMINANTS UNDER THE UMBRELLA OF ONE HEALTH CONCEPT

Sobre el Proyecto

As a result of the long-lasted collaboration of researchers from two different disciplines (ecotoxicology and environmental analytical chemistry) the present proposal wants to achieve common objectives regarding the holistic assessment of environmental health. More precisely, the main aim of this project is to carry out a holistic study to understand the presence, and effects produced by different pollutants (past and actual) and the interactions among them in critical natural scenarios. Following the main postulates of One Health Concept these critical scenarios are defined according to the dissimilar presence of specific contaminants and their combinations (past and present industrial activity in urban environments, antibiotic release by hospital and WWTP emissaries, and chronic pollution by domestic activities in protected areas). Past pollution events and their toxicity will be addressed aided by cores in the different scenarios. In summary, this proposal will allow, based on a comprehensive diagnosis, to propose strategies for the control and attenuation of the potential effects exerted by a series of pollutants and their interaction.

The specific objectives of the proposal are listed below:

  • 1.- To make progress in the methodology to measure accurately the concentration of emerging contaminants in environmental samples, with special emphasis in the analysis of microplastics because they act as vectors of other pollutants.
  • 2.- To determine the presence and concentration levels of microplastics, antibiotics, Li and REEs in different environmental matrices from selected scenarios to assess their environmental and human health risks and to better understand their interactions and mitigate their potential impacts (Note: The presence, persistence, transformation of antibiotics and their interactions with other contaminants and living beings (microbiota, invertebrates, ...) will be assessed in relation with hospital and WWTP sources including submarine emissaries).
  • 3.- To investigate the role of biofilm on the interaction between MPs and chemical pollutants (Li, REEs and antibiotics).
  • 4.-To analyze the effect of the contaminants in the phylogenetic bacterial composition and the role of MPs as reservoirs of antibiotic resistance bacteria and genes.
